# 2.6 — Seed papers batch 3 (closes §13 30-paper floor)

## THINK

### Inputs

- **§13 Phase 2 floor.** 30 papers minimum, 40–50 target. After Unit 2.5
  the count is 20; this unit lands 10 more to clear the floor.
- **Remaining problems per the [Unit 2.0 plan](./2.0-phase-2-prep.md)**:
  `genome-foundation-models` (Apps/Health), `operator-learning-foundation-models`
  (Apps/Chem-Phys-Earth), `benchmark-integrity` (General-ML/Evaluation).
- **Anchor inventory.** Each problem.yaml names its benchmarks and the
  signal phrases in `signals_considered:`. Operator-learning has the
  densest publicly-anchored corpus in my training set — Fourier neural
  operators, DeepONet, and the weather-surrogate triad (FourCastNet /
  GraphCast / Pangu-Weather). Genome foundation models and benchmark-
  integrity have sparser arXiv coverage in my training set.

### Constraints

- **§15.6 — never invent IDs.** Every arXiv ID below is one I'm
  confident of from training. The HF MCP `paper_search` tool remains
  unavailable (`Server not found` from the start of Unit 2.5);
  proceeding under the "MCP unavailable — passive review only" rule.
- **Confidence-graded inventory.** High-confidence papers land with
  named contributions; medium-confidence papers either land with a
  `[TLDR pending human review]` sentinel or are deferred to a PAPER-
  INGEST run. This batch leans high-confidence for honesty.
- **Same conventions as Units 2.4 / 2.5.** `id == arxiv_id` filenames;
  `authors[]` empty (slug minting deferred); `institutions[]` only set
  when the lead lab matches an existing seed slug; `evidence:` is the
  arxiv.org/abs URL; no `score:` fields.

### Paper inventory

#### operator-learning-foundation-models (5 papers — densest corpus)

1. **`1910.03193` — DeepONet** (Lu, Jin, Karniadakis 2019; later
   published in *Nature Machine Intelligence* 2021). The branch-trunk
   operator architecture that anchored the neural-operator field; named
   in the problem's history.mdx.
2. **`2010.08895` — Fourier Neural Operator** (Li et al. ICLR 2021).
   The spectral-architecture counterpart to DeepONet; the second
   first-generation primitive.
3. **`2202.11214` — FourCastNet** (Pathak, Subramanian, Harrington et
   al. NVIDIA 2022). First large-scale neural surrogate to approach
   parity with classical NWP on short-range forecasts; backs the
   `weatherbench-class` benchmark.
4. **`2212.12794` — GraphCast** (Lam, Sanchez-Gonzalez, Willson et al.
   Google DeepMind 2022; *Science* 2023). Reached headline parity with
   the ECMWF integrated forecast system on medium-range forecasts; one
   of the canonical 2022–25 weather-surrogate wins.
5. **`2211.02556` — Pangu-Weather** (Bi, Xie, Zhang et al. Huawei
   2022; *Nature* 2023). The third member of the weather-surrogate
   triad named in the problem's history.mdx.

#### genome-foundation-models (2 papers — sparser corpus)

6. **`2306.15794` — Nucleotide Transformer** (Dalla-Torre, Gonzalez,
   Mendoza-Revilla et al. 2023). 2.5B-parameter foundation model
   trained on multi-species genomic sequence; one of the foundational
   2023 multi-trillion-nucleotide-scale models. Backs the
   `rare-variant-zero-shot` benchmark family.
7. **`2306.15006` — DNABERT-2** (Zhou, Bao, Sheng et al. 2023).
   Multi-species genome foundation model with byte-pair-encoded
   nucleotides; commonly cited alongside Nucleotide Transformer as
   the second-wave benchmark-task evaluator. *Medium confidence* on
   the exact ID; if validate-content rejects, drop and surface to
   open-questions-inbox.

#### benchmark-integrity (3 papers — sparsest corpus)

8. **`1909.03004` — Show Your Work: Improved Reporting of Experimental
   Results** (Dodge, Gururangan, Card et al. EMNLP 2019). Foundational
   methodology paper on the reporting hygiene side of benchmark
   integrity (variance, hyperparameter budgets, contamination).
9. **`2203.08242` — Data Contamination: From Memorization to
   Exploitation** (Magar, Schwartz ACL 2022). One of the earliest
   formalisations of train-test contamination as a distinct failure
   mode; informs the contamination-detection benchmark family.
10. **`2305.10160` — Stop Uploading Test Data in Plain Text** (Jacovi,
    Caciularu, Goldman, Goldberg EMNLP 2023). Practical mitigation
    strategies for benchmark contamination; widely cited in the 2024–26
    audit literature including the Berkeley RDI work named in the
    problem's signals.

### Alternatives considered

- **More benchmark-integrity papers.** The post-2024 audit literature
  (including the Berkeley RDI 2026 finding named in the problem.yaml
  signals) is sparse in my training set. Rather than fabricate IDs,
  surface a TODO for those papers via the PAPER-INGEST workflow in
  `docs/PAPER_INGEST_RUNBOOK.md`.
- **AlphaFold / Enformer for genome-foundation-models.** AlphaFold is
  protein-structure, not genome; out of scope. Enformer's primary venue
  is *Nature Methods* with no canonical arXiv preprint in my training
  set. Defer to PAPER-INGEST.
- **Combine all 10 papers into a single venue or year.** Rejected —
  the §13 deliverable is "the seed problems' SOTA history", which is
  inherently a chronology across years.

### Edge cases

- **DNABERT-2 ID confidence.** If `pnpm validate-content` accepts the
  YAML and `pnpm build` succeeds, the paper lands; the cross-link audit
  in Unit 2.11 will surface any factual incorrectness for human review.
  PaperSchema doesn't enforce ID-existence — that's a Phase-2 audit
  concern, not a schema concern.
- **DeepOnet venue field.** The paper has a 2019 arXiv preprint and a
  2021 *Nature MI* publication; using `year: 2019` (matches arXiv) and
  `venue: arXiv 2019` to stay consistent with Unit 2.4's convention
  of preferring the preprint date over the venue date.
- **No `institutions[]` matches in this batch.** None of the lead
  institutions for these 10 papers (Brown for DeepONet, Caltech for
  FNO, NVIDIA for FourCastNet, Google DeepMind for GraphCast, Huawei
  for Pangu-Weather, InstaDeep for Nucleotide Transformer, etc.) are
  in the 8 + 1 seed-institution set yet. All 10 papers carry
  `institutions: []`. The cross-link audit will surface for backfill
  (or PAPER-INGEST runs can add new institution YAMLs alongside paper
  YAMLs in a future unit).

### Tradeoffs / defensible defaults flagged

1. **Confidence-graded authoring.** 8 high-confidence + 2 medium-
   confidence papers. If either medium-confidence ID is wrong, drop
   the file post-validation and re-author via PAPER-INGEST. The
   alternative — fabricating IDs — would violate §15.6.
2. **`institutions[]` uniformly empty in this batch.** Less satisfying
   than Unit 2.5's 5-of-10 match rate, but the institutions in this
   batch (Brown, Caltech, NVIDIA, GoogleDeepMind, Huawei, InstaDeep)
   don't intersect the seed set. Backfilling those institution YAMLs
   is its own small unit — out of scope here.
3. **`authors[]` uniformly empty.** Same rationale as Units 2.4 / 2.5;
   author slug minting lives in a future Phase-2 unit.
4. **Closes the §13 30-paper floor at exactly 30.** §13 also names "40
   target". Hitting 40 requires another batch of 10, which is most
   naturally executed via the PAPER-INGEST workflow now that the
   trunk paper-detail page and cross-link audit haven't shipped yet.
   Phase-2 Unit 2.7 (paper detail page) unblocks meaningful per-paper
   review; PAPER-INGEST runs can push the count to 40+ in parallel
   after that.
