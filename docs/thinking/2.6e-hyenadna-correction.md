# 2.6e — HyenaDNA correction for 2306.15794.yaml

Phase-2 hygiene follow-on. Fixes a data-integrity bug flagged in
[2.6d-ror-id-backfill-original-institutions.md](./2.6d-ror-id-backfill-original-institutions.md#surfaced-findings-for-the-user--future-curator):
`content/papers/2306.15794.yaml` claims to be "The Nucleotide
Transformer" (Dalla-Torre et al., InstaDeep) but arXiv ID `2306.15794`
is in fact **HyenaDNA** (Nguyen et al., Stanford / UC Berkeley / Mila).
The Unit-2.6 seed conflated the two papers.

## THINK

### Problem restatement

The paper YAML at [2306.15794.yaml](../../content/papers/2306.15794.yaml)
currently asserts:

- `id: "2306.15794"` (the arXiv ID for HyenaDNA)
- `arxiv_id: "2306.15794"` (same)
- `title: "The Nucleotide Transformer: ..."` (a DIFFERENT paper —
  Dalla-Torre et al., on bioRxiv 10.1101/2023.01.11.523679)
- `institutions: [instadeep]` (Nucleotide-Transformer-correct; HyenaDNA-wrong)
- `tldr:` describing multi-species genomic-sequence pretraining at the
  scale of the Nucleotide Transformer (Nucleotide-Transformer-correct;
  HyenaDNA's contribution was long-context single-nucleotide modeling)
- `contributions[0].benchmark_id: rare-variant-zero-shot` (the
  Nucleotide-Transformer-defined benchmark; HyenaDNA evaluated on it
  downstream but did not define it)

WebFetch on `https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.15794` confirms the actual
paper at that ID is "HyenaDNA: Long-Range Genomic Sequence Modeling at
Single Nucleotide Resolution" by Eric Nguyen et al.

### Inputs

- **Authoritative title + author list**: WebFetch on
  `https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.15794` (this session). Title and 13-author
  list captured verbatim above.
- **Authoritative institution attribution**: WebFetch on
  `https://hazyresearch.stanford.edu/blog/2023-06-29-hyena-dna`
  identifies HyenaDNA as a Hazy Research (Stanford) project with one
  author (Marjan Faizi) at UC Berkeley. Compute support from Google
  Cloud / Together / SynTensor is acknowledged but those are donors,
  not authoring institutions.
- **Existing institution slugs**: both `stanford-university` and
  `uc-berkeley` are in `content/institutions/` already (Unit 2.1).
  Mila / Université de Montréal is not in the registry; Yoshua Bengio's
  affiliation goes unrepresented in this unit but is recoverable via a
  later author/affiliation pass.
- **`rare-variant-zero-shot` benchmark** declared in
  [genome-foundation-models/problem.yaml](../../content/problems/genome-foundation-models/problem.yaml)
  is the Nucleotide-Transformer-defined surface, not HyenaDNA's. After
  this fix, no paper in the repo contributes to it. The benchmark
  declaration in problem.yaml stays; only the paper-side claim is
  withdrawn.

### Constraints

- **§15.6 — no fabrication.** Every field set in the corrected YAML
  must trace to a primary source — arXiv abstract page for title +
  authors, Hazy Research blog post for institutional attribution.
- **`exactOptionalPropertyTypes: true`** in tsconfig. Where a field has
  no attested value (e.g. `benchmark_id` on the corrected HyenaDNA
  contribution), omit the key rather than write `undefined`.
- **Cross-link audit must stay green**. After the fix:
  - `paper-problem-fk`: contribution's `problem_slug:
    genome-foundation-models` remains valid (slug exists).
  - `paper-institution-fk`: `stanford-university` + `uc-berkeley` both
    exist in `content/institutions/`. No dangling ref.
  - `paper-author-fk`: `authors: []` remains (consistent with the
    batch-3 papers; a future author-backfill unit will populate).
- **`authors: []` deliberately preserved.** The 10 Unit-2.6 papers all
  carry `authors: []` (see 2.5b's parallel-session work on the batch-2
  set). Adding HyenaDNA's 13 authors here would (a) duplicate the
  parallel session's pattern in one of two paper batches; (b) require
  minting ~13 author YAMLs whose slugs may overlap with future batch-3
  rosters. Let the next-in-rhythm batch-3 author-backfill unit handle
  the full 10 papers' rosters uniformly.
- **InstaDeep institution stays.** Removing the `instadeep` slug from
  this paper's `institutions[]` leaves
  [`content/institutions/instadeep.yaml`](../../content/institutions/instadeep.yaml)
  orphan (no incoming refs). The cross-link audit does not flag
  orphan institutions, and a future curator pass that re-introduces
  a Nucleotide-Transformer paper (keyed by bioRxiv DOI) re-anchors
  the slug. Deleting it now would lose that re-anchor option.

### Alternatives considered

1. **Option (a) from 2.6d's findings — re-target 2306.15794.yaml to
   HyenaDNA only.** Chosen. Minimal change, fully attestable, leaves
   exactly one orphan institution as documented exception.
2. **Option (c) — keep InstaDeep referenced by minting a separate
   Nucleotide-Transformer paper with a non-arXiv ID.** Rejected for
   this unit — would require:
   - A non-arXiv `id` (bioRxiv DOI `10.1101/2023.01.11.523679`), which
     the paper-detail-page logic in Unit 2.7 may assume == arxiv_id.
     Untested code path on the rendering side.
   - Minting Dalla-Torre et al.'s 22 authors, also untested for
     non-arXiv-keyed papers.
   The right home for this is a dedicated "non-arXiv paper key" unit
   in the next batch.
3. **Update venue from `arXiv 2023` to `NeurIPS 2023`.** HyenaDNA was
   indeed presented at NeurIPS 2023, but this fact is not attested by
   the arXiv abstract page (arXiv pages don't list venue) and the
   GitHub README's BibTeX entry I fetched was truncated. Defer venue
   update until a primary-source attestation lands; keep `arXiv 2023`
   for now.
4. **Add Mila as an institution.** Mila is Yoshua Bengio's lab; Bengio
   is a senior author. But Mila has no slug in `content/institutions/`
   yet, and minting it as a side-effect of a content-correction unit
   blurs scope. A future per-author / per-institution pass adds Mila
   when other Mila-affiliated papers join the repo.
5. **Drop the `rare-variant-zero-shot` benchmark from problem.yaml
   since no paper contributes to it after this fix.** Rejected — the
   benchmark declaration is editorial intent (problem.yaml lists the
   benchmarks the problem *should* track), independent of which papers
   currently report on it. A future Nucleotide-Transformer paper unit
   re-attaches.

### Edge cases & failure modes

1. **InstaDeep institution becomes orphan.** Documented; no audit error.
   Re-attached on a future Nucleotide-Transformer paper unit.
2. **`rare-variant-zero-shot` benchmark has no contributing paper.**
   Same — benchmark declaration is fine without contributions; the
   leaderboard page renders empty for it.
3. **Future batch-3 author-backfill unit re-mints HyenaDNA authors.**
   `authors: []` here matches every other Unit-2.6 paper; a uniform
   batch-3 pass picks up all 10 papers consistently. No collision.
4. **CRLF on commit.** Standard Windows pattern; if `git diff` shows
   empty but `git status --short` shows `M`, re-`git add` and the
   commit succeeds (per the session-handoff "Known wrinkles").
