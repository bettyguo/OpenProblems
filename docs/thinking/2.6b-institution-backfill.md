# 2.6b — Institution backfill for Units 2.5 / 2.6 seed papers

## THINK

### Inputs

- **Units 2.5 / 2.6 landed 20 papers** with a uniform-empty
  `institutions[]` policy (only papers whose lead lab matched an
  existing seed-institution slug got a populated field). That left 6
  papers with `institutions: []` whose lead labs are well-known and
  easy to author:
  - `1910.03193` DeepONet — Brown University (Karniadakis group)
  - `2010.08895` Fourier Neural Operator — Caltech (Anandkumar group)
  - `2202.11214` FourCastNet — NVIDIA
  - `2211.02556` Pangu-Weather — Huawei (Cloud BU / Noah's Ark Lab)
  - `2306.15794` Nucleotide Transformer — InstaDeep
  - `2404.06654` RULER — NVIDIA
- **Cross-link audit (Unit 2.11)** will surface every dangling
  paper→institution slug. Pre-emptive backfill shrinks the audit
  backlog and gives the `/institutions/[slug]` pages real data.
- **InstitutionSchema** (`lib/schemas/institution.ts`): `slug`,
  `display_name`, optional `country`, `type` ∈ {academic, industry,
  government, nonprofit, other}, `homepage` (URL), `ror_id` (ROR
  regex).

### Constraints

- **5 new institution slugs**: `brown-university`, `caltech`,
  `nvidia`, `huawei`, `instadeep`. None overlaps with the existing 9
  (`anthropic`, `google-deepmind`, `meta-fair`, `microsoft-research`,
  `mit`, `openai`, `princeton-university`, `stanford-university`,
  `uc-berkeley`).
- **6 paper YAML edits**: add `institutions:` array entry only.
  Don't reorder fields, don't reformat unrelated lines.
- **No fabricated ROR IDs** (§15.6). Leave `ror_id` off where I
  can't recall it confidently. All 5 institutions have public
  homepages I'm certain of.
- **No Velite config changes.** `institutions` collection already
  exists; new YAMLs land via existing glob.
- **No new lib/app code.** This is content + content edits only —
  zero collision risk with the parallel session's Unit 2.8 work in
  `lib/content/load-papers-index.ts` and `components/papers-index/`.

### Alternatives considered

- **Add ROR IDs too.** Rejected — fabricating IDs violates §15.6.
  ROR backfill can land in a follow-up content commit once a curator
  pulls them from `ror.org`.
- **Defer to Unit 2.11 (cross-link audit) to surface and then human-
  backfill.** Rejected — these 5 institutions are obvious enough that
  doing them now saves the audit pass entirely; it also gives the
  `/institutions/[slug]` SSG route 5 more prerendered paths (total
  goes from 9 to 14).
- **Add affiliated-author backfill in the same commit.** Rejected —
  out of scope; authors backfill is its own future content unit
  (Phase-2 plan didn't separately enumerate it but it's the natural
  pair to this).

### Tradeoffs / defensible defaults flagged

1. **`type` field**: `industry` for NVIDIA, Huawei, InstaDeep;
   `academic` for Brown and Caltech. Standard categorisation.
2. **`country` field**: US for NVIDIA / Brown / Caltech, CN for
   Huawei (HQ Shenzhen), TN for InstaDeep (Tunisia-founded, now
   also UK / France offices — use TN to preserve founding origin).
3. **`homepage`**: top-level corporate / institutional homepage in
   every case; not the research lab subdomain.
