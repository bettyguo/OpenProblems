---
title: History
summary: From the 2020–23 small-model circuit era, through the 2024–25 SAE wave, to the 2026 status where frontier-scale faithfulness remains open and the auditing use case is still aspirational.
---

The 2020–2022 period established the small-model circuit playbook: identify an algorithmic capability, propose a candidate circuit, validate it with activation patching and ablation. Induction heads (the in-context learning circuit), indirect-object-identification, and a handful of toy-model circuits were the headline results. The methods worked because the models were small and the tasks were narrow.

The 2023 transition was scale: induction-head-style claims at GPT-2 / GPT-3 scale were attempted with activation-patching variants and attribution-graph methods. The faithfulness picture became more nuanced — claims that held on small models did not always survive ablation at larger scale, and the human cost per claim grew.

The 2024–2025 inflection was sparse autoencoders. SAE training over residual-stream activations produced large dictionaries of features that were interpretable enough to label and probe, and SAEBench standardised their evaluation. The 2026 status, after a wave of mixed verdicts in safety and alignment status reports, is that SAEs gave the field a useful representation but did not close the gap to deployment-time auditing. Faithful, high-coverage claims at frontier scale — and especially circuits relevant to deceptive alignment or capability gating — remain the open frontier.
