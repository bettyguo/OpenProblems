---
title: History
summary: From the 2019–21 first-generation neural operators (DeepONet, Fourier neural operator), through the 2022–25 in-distribution parity wins on weather and fluids, to the 2026 frontier of cross-regime generalisation.
---

The neural-operator program crystallised around 2019–2021 with two architectural primitives: DeepONet (a branch-trunk operator architecture with universal-approximation guarantees) and the Fourier neural operator (a spectral architecture that learns operators in frequency space). Early demonstrations on canonical PDE families — Burgers, Darcy, Navier–Stokes — showed that learned operators could match per-instance numerical solvers at much lower inference cost on the distribution they were trained on.

The 2022–2025 wave brought scaling and applied wins. Atmospheric models trained on ERA5 reanalyses (the FourCastNet / GraphCast / Pangu-Weather lineage) reached headline parity with classical numerical-weather-prediction systems on short-to-medium-range forecasts, at inference costs orders of magnitude lower. Fluid-dynamics surrogates were deployed in industrial CFD pipelines for screening designs.

The 2025–26 status sharpened the picture. In-distribution wins are routine; out-of-distribution generalisation is the binding constraint. Extreme weather events that fall outside the training distribution are predicted less reliably than in-distribution events; parameter regimes the model did not see are not extrapolated to faithfully; surrogate fidelity on novel geometries is poor. The frontier task is the foundation-model version: a single pretrained operator that transfers across PDE families and across regimes within a family. As of 2026 no such model exists at industrial fidelity.
